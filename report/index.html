Multi-Frame Denoising

A burst-capture pipeline that solves the ISO–noise trade-off in low-light photography through image registration and adaptive multi-frame fusion.

Pipeline stages: 5  ·  Denoising methods: 4  ·  Input format: Canon CR3 RGGB Bayer  ·  Stack: NumPy · OpenCV · SciPy
01 — Problem Statement

The Fundamental Dilemma of Night Photography

Capturing clear images at night requires sufficient photon count per pixel. Photographers face a three-way conflict between shutter speed, aperture, and ISO sensitivity. Standard practice forces a choice between two bad outcomes.

🌃
Long shutter → Motion blur

Increasing exposure time accumulates more photons, but any subject movement or camera shake during the exposure interval integrates into a spatial smear across pixels — irreversibly destroying high-frequency detail.

Blur artefact
🔆
High ISO → Sensor noise

Amplifying the sensor signal (high ISO) keeps shutter speeds short enough to freeze motion, but the analog amplifier boosts both signal and read noise. The resulting image exhibits strong shot noise and fixed-pattern noise.

Noise artefact
📷
Burst capture → Our solution

Using burst mode at high ISO captures many short-exposure frames that each freeze motion. Aligning these frames and fusing them statistically reduces noise by up to √N while recovering the original scene signal.

Stage 1 — RAW Loading (pipeline/raw_loader.py)

Canon CR3 files are decoded with rawpy (wraps libraw). The raw sensor values are extracted as a 2-D Bayer mosaic (RGGB pattern) and normalised from 14-bit integers to float32 [0,1]. Black-level subtraction and white-level normalisation are applied automatically. A dcraw shell-out provides a fallback for CR3 files that rawpy cannot parse.

bayer = load_raw_bayer("IMG_0042.CR3") # → float32 (H×W)

Stage 2 — Image Registration (pipeline/registration.py)

Three algorithms are available: ECC (Enhanced Correlation Coefficient, sub-pixel translation via iterative gradient descent), ORB feature matching (RANSAC-based partial affine for rotation/scale), and phase correlation (FFT O(N log N), fastest). The sharpest frame is automatically selected as the reference. Registration is performed on the Bayer mosaic directly — demosaicing before registration would smear colour edges.

aligned, transforms = register_burst(frames, method="ecc")

Stage 3 — Denoising (pipeline/denoising.py)

Four selectable methods (see the Methods section below). All operate on the aligned Bayer-domain stack. The statistical signal-to-noise improvement scales as √N for uncorrelated Gaussian noise, where N is the number of aligned frames.

result = denoise_burst(aligned, method="weighted")

Stage 4 — ISP Post-processing (pipeline/isp.py)

Converts the denoised Bayer mosaic to a viewable RGB image: (1) Demosaicing — bilinear, edge-aware, or VNG interpolation to reconstruct missing colour samples; (2) White balance — grey-world or manual gain per channel; (3) Reinhard tone mapping — compresses HDR linear values; (4) Gamma encoding — 2.2 power law for display.

rgb_u8 = run_isp(denoised_bayer, demosaic_method="edgeaware")

Stage 5 — Output and Metrics

Each method produces a PNG alongside a JSON metrics file containing PSNR (dB), SSIM, processing time, and estimated noise σ. A side-by-side comparison strip is saved automatically. The pipeline is invoked as a CLI:

python run_pipeline.py --input_dir ./data/ --method all --reg_method ecc

Simple Temporal Mean

Computes the arithmetic mean of all N aligned frames at each pixel. For i.i.d. Gaussian noise with variance σ², averaging N frames reduces variance to σ²/N — an SNR gain of √N in amplitude. Fast, interpretable, and a strong baseline.

μ(x,y) = (1/N) Σᵢ fᵢ(x,y)

Variance-Weighted Average

Computes a per-pixel inverse-variance weighted average. Pixels that exhibit high local variance across the burst (motion residuals, noise spikes) receive lower weight, preserving the contribution of well-aligned, low-noise pixels. Robust to partial registration failures.

μ_w(x,y) = Σᵢ wᵢ·fᵢ / Σᵢ wᵢ  where wᵢ = 1/(σ²_local + ε)
Handles outlier frames Local variance estimation adds overhead Spatially adaptive Window size is a free parameter

Frequency-Domain Wiener Filter

First collapses the burst with temporal mean to reduce noise variance, then applies a 2-D Wiener filter in the DFT domain. The Wiener transfer function attenuates frequency bands where noise power exceeds signal power, while preserving texture-rich frequency components.

H(u,v) = |S(u,v)|² / (|S(u,v)|² + σ²_n)
Excellent texture preservation Assumes stationarity Principled SNR optimality Ringing at sharp edges

Non-Local Means (NLM)

Leverages the self-similarity of natural images: each pixel is denoised using a weighted average over all pixels whose local neighbourhood patch is similar. Similarity is measured by the Euclidean distance of patch intensities. Applied via OpenCV's fastNlMeansDenoising on the temporally averaged frame.

NLM(x) = Σ_y w(x,y)·f(y)   where w ∝ exp(−‖P_x−P_y‖²/h²)

05 — Expected Results

What We Expect to Demonstrate

01
Successful burst registration across different motion levels

ECC alignment should achieve sub-pixel accuracy (< 0.5 px residual) for tripod-mounted bursts. For handheld shots (1–5 px jitter), phase correlation should converge within 1 pixel. We expect > 95% of frame pairs to align successfully.

02
Noise reduced by ~√N across all methods at minimum

Even the simplest mean fusion should deliver the theoretical √8 ≈ 2.83× noise reduction (~4.5 dB PSNR improvement) on static scenes. This validates the registration quality and serves as an interpretable lower bound.

03
NLM achieves best perceptual quality, frequency domain preserves textures

We expect NLM to score highest on SSIM due to edge-preserving denoising. The frequency-domain Wiener filter should outperform NLM on synthetic textures (repetitive patterns) while showing ringing near high-contrast edges.

04
Visible improvement in final RGB output

The side-by-side comparison between a single noisy frame and the full pipeline output should show clearly reduced grain in flat areas (sky, walls) and improved readability of fine details (text, foliage) without significant loss of sharpness.

05
Graceful degradation with more motion / fewer frames

We will also demonstrate the pipeline's limitations: large inter-frame motion (> 10 px) causes registration failure artifacts, and burst sizes below N = 4 show diminishing denoising returns. The weighted method should degrade more gracefully than simple mean under these stress conditions.
